The company

Built by someone
who ran the banquet.

DigiVenue was founded by Rohit Nate — a Maharashtra-based hospitality operator with over 13 years of real experience inside banquet halls, catering businesses and wedding operations. Not theoretical startup knowledge. The kind that comes from actually being inside the business.

13 years inside the industry

Not a startup idea.
An operational reality.

Rohit Nate, founder of DigiVenue · In a Mumbai banquet hall

Rohit Nate didn't study the wedding industry from the outside. He managed banquet halls. He handled wedding bookings. He coordinated decorators, navigated vendor royalty disputes, dealt with GST invoicing chaos, fielded walk-in enquiries, and ran operations from WhatsApp chats and paper diary systems.

He watched good banquet halls — banquets with beautiful interiors, experienced staff, years of goodwill and real operational strength — stay invisible online. Families would search, find an inactive Google page with six-year-old photos, and quietly choose another venue. The banquet never knew.

SmartOS began as a system he built for his own venue operations. DigiStories began as visibility work he started doing for banquets that were losing bookings they should have won. He realised the same problems affected thousands of banquet halls across India — and that nobody had built anything specifically for them.

A banquet hall can spend crores on interiors and still lose bookings because it looks inactive online. Families decide before they call. Most banquets never realise this is happening to them.
The gap nobody filled

Every platform was built for someone else.

The Indian wedding industry has produced many platforms. None of them were built for the venue operator.

Listing portals

WedMeGood. Weddingz.in. BookEventz. Built for couples. Built for commissions. Venue operators pay to be listed, pay per enquiry, and have no control over how they are presented or trusted. The relationship is extractive — not supportive.

What operators actually need

Tools to track offline leads. Systems to manage bookings and payments. Help building digital trust over time. A partner who understands the real operational chaos of running a banquet — and has lived it.

The invisible rejection problem

Families today discover venues through Instagram, Google photos, reviews and reels — before they ever make a call. Most banquets are operationally strong but digitally silent during this critical discovery journey. They lose bookings they never knew they were being considered for.

What DigiVenue does differently

DigiVenue does not list venues. It transforms how venues appear online, builds digital trust over time, and provides operational tools built around how Indian banquet businesses actually work — from enquiry to invoice.

How we operate

A hospitality company,
not a software startup.

DigiVenue is not a social media agency. Not a CRM company. Not a listing portal. It is building India's first hospitality-native operating ecosystem — and the way it grows reflects the values of the industry it serves.

Never pitch.
Always show.

The work speaks before any conversation does. We show what transformation looks like — with real venues and real numbers — so banquet owners can see exactly what they are considering before they decide anything.

Never sell.
Always help.

Every interaction should leave a venue owner with more clarity about their situation — whether or not they work with us. The founder approaches every conversation as a hospitality consultant, not a salesperson.

Never push.
Always invite.

Growth happens through trust-first hospitality relationships. We grow the way good venues grow — through reputation, through results, and through the kind of trust that takes time to earn and is worth keeping.

The ecosystem

Five layers.
One operating ecosystem.

DigiVenue is not a single product. It is a set of interconnected layers — each built for a different part of running a banquet business. Most venues start with DigiStories. The rest grows with them, as they grow.

DigiStories
Launching now

Banquet visibility transformation. Instagram, Google presence, real event photography, reels, reviews and trust-building content — everything a venue needs to appear active and trusted during the family discovery journey.

SmartOS
Prototype stage

Venue management system built for Indian banquet operators. Enquiry tracking, booking management, calendar conflicts, payments, GST invoicing, vendor royalty tracking and operational workflows.

DigiNetwork
In development

Hospitality relationship infrastructure. Connecting banquet halls with trusted caterers, decorators, photographers and planners through relationship-based referrals — not directory listings or commission arrangements.

DigiMarket
In development

Hospitality commerce layer. Sourcing decor, supplies and event infrastructure for banquet halls at better rates — through the DigiVenue network of trusted hospitality suppliers.

DigiShaadi
Concept stage

Wedding discovery ecosystem. A venue-trust layer where families can find real Indian banquets, see real wedding photos, and make informed decisions — without commission-driven listing portals.
