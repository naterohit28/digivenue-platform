Why we started this
“We know what a 1,000-plate wedding feels like. We know what happens when the decor team is two hours late. We started DigiVenue because running a good banquet isn't enough anymore — you have to look like a good banquet online, too.”
— Founders, DigiVenue
Where we come from

Fifty years in Mumbai's banquet business.

We're not a software company. Our family has run catering and banquet operations across Mumbai for over fifty years. We grew up around kitchen rotations, decor teams, baraat timings, and the long phone calls with families about menu changes the week before a wedding.

Over the last few years, we kept seeing the same thing — beautiful banquets with proud histories were losing bookings to newer, smaller banquets down the road. It wasn't the food. It wasn't the location. The newer banquet just looked more active online: fresh photos every week, recent reviews, a Google page that responded the same day.

Owners didn't need another agency promising likes that don't bring bookings. They needed someone who would quietly run their Google page, their Instagram and their reviews — week after week — so the banquet they've spent a lifetime building actually looks the part online. That's why we started DigiVenue.

How we work

Three things we don't compromise on.

We're at your banquet on event days

We don't ask you to send us photos. Our team is at your function — Saturday weddings, Sunday receptions, peak-season nights — capturing the mandap, the decor and the moments that matter. Real photos, every week.

We only do Mumbai banquets

Chembur, Dadar, Ghatkopar, Thane, Vashi. We know what families in this city search for, which weeks of the season decide the year, and how the locality competes. That focus is the whole point.

We show you the problem first — no pressure

We start with a free check of how your banquet looks online — what's working, what's missing, what's quietly costing you bookings. If it makes sense to work together after that, we'll tell you. If it doesn't, we'll say so.
