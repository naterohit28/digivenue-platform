# Venue Health Report — Design Specification v1

A premium, founder-friendly consulting report generated from the intelligence stack. **One artifact, ~7 A4 pages, readable on a phone, designed to be kept and re-read at home.**

**Design principles**
- **McKinsey structure, hospitality warmth.** Pyramid principle (answer first → evidence → plan), but plain respectful language — never "operational readiness," always "how your office handles inquiries."
- **Premium visual system (reuse the brand tokens):** warm ivory `#FAF7F2`, rich charcoal `#1F1D1C`, restrained vermillion `#D94822`, champagne gold `#C5A059`; Playfair Display (headings) + Inter (body); 0px radius (architectural); generous whitespace.
- **One idea per page.** Each page answers one question and ends with a takeaway line.
- **Honest by design:** ranges not promises; every number traceable; a standing disclaimer.

**Customer-facing language map** (internal → report): Discoverability→"How findable you are" · Trust→"How much families trust you" · Conversion→"How inquiries become bookings" · Operations→"How your office runs" · DMI→"Overall Health Score" · Leadership pillar → *omitted (internal only)*.

Full-bleed ivory, gold hairline border, a single atmospheric venue photo at low opacity. Sets the "this was made for me" tone.

### PAGE 1 — Executive Summary  *(the answer, first)*
**Source:** DMI + OSE + REC headline.
- **One-paragraph verdict** (3–4 sentences, plain): where they stand, the single biggest leak, the size of the prize, the first move.
- **Three hero stat cards** (horizontal):
  `Overall Health 19/100 (🔴)` · `Revenue at stake ₹5–10L/mo` · `Biggest opportunity: get found on Google`
- **Takeaway line** (italic gold): *"You run a strong hall offline. The gap is purely online — and it's fixable."*
- **Chart:** none (restraint — summary is words + 3 numbers).

### PAGE 2 — Overall Health Grade
**Source:** DMI six pillars + VMF stage.
- **Grade gauge** (top): semicircular gauge 0–100 with the DMI needle + letter/colour band.
- **Pillar scorecard table:**
  | Pillar (plain label) | Score | Band |
  |---|---|---|
  | How findable you are | 25 | 🔴 |
  | How much families trust you | 16 | 🔴 |
  | How inquiries become bookings | 29 | 🔴 |
  | How your office runs | 9 | 🔴 |
  | Measuring what works | 6 | 🔴 |
- **Chart:** horizontal bar chart of the 5 pillars (colour = RAG), sorted weakest-first.
- **Maturity ribbon:** `Traditional → [Semi-Digital] → Modern → Intelligent` with current stage marked and "weakest link: your office systems."
- **Takeaway:** one line naming the stage and what unlocks the next.

### PAGE 3 — Competitive Benchmark
**Source:** CIE.
- **Bullet-bar chart per metric** (the signature visual): for each of Reviews, Rating, Photos, Instagram, Website — a horizontal track showing **You ▮**, **Area average │**, **Leader ▮**, so the gap is instantly visual.
- **Benchmark table:**
  | Metric | You | Area avg | Area leader | Your percentile |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | Google reviews | 34 | 325 | 2,100 | 18th 🔴 |
  | Instagram activity | 0.2/wk | 1.8/wk | 5/wk | 12th 🔴 |
- **Competitive Index dial** + position word (Laggard / Average / Leader).
- **Takeaway:** *"In your area you currently sit in the bottom fifth on the things families check first."*

### PAGE 4 — Top Weaknesses & Revenue Leakage
**Source:** REC weaknesses + OSE.
- **Top 3 weaknesses** as ranked cards (cause → plain consequence): "Unclaimed Google → families can't find you," etc.
- **Revenue leakage waterfall** (the money page): a waterfall/stacked bar from "Current bookings" up through recoverable bookings by cause to "Potential" — ending at the ₹ range.
- **Leakage table:**
  | Leak | Missed inquiries/mo | → bookings | → ₹/mo (conservative) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Missing reviews | 1.2 | … | … |
  | No website | 1.1 | … | … |
  | **Total** | | | **₹5.2L** |
- **Confidence note** beside the ₹ figure (e.g. "based on the numbers you shared · estimate").
- **Takeaway:** the conservative ₹/year figure, framed as "what one extra booking a month is worth."

### PAGE 5 — Quick Wins
**Source:** REC (sorted by Priority = bang-per-effort).
- **Quick-wins table** (3–5 rows): low-effort, high-confidence first.
  | Quick win | Effort | +Health | Worth/yr | Confidence |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | Add WhatsApp button everywhere | Low | +6 | ₹6.2L | 59% |
  | Claim your Google profile | Low | +4 | ₹3.8L | 75% |
- **"Start here" callout:** the single #1 priority action, gold box, with the one sentence why.
- **Chart:** an effort-vs-impact scatter (2×2) so "do these first" is visual (top-left = high impact, low effort).
- **Takeaway:** *"Three of these can be done in the first two weeks — no cost to you beyond our work."*

### PAGE 6 — 90-Day Action Plan
**Source:** REC + VMF roadmap, phased.
- **Timeline (Gantt-style)** across 3 phases:
  ```
  Phase 1 · Days 1–14   Foundation     [████░░░░░░]  Google, photos, WhatsApp
  Phase 2 · Days 15–45  Distribution   [░░░░██████]  reels, reviews, inquiry tracking
  Phase 3 · Days 45–90  Convert        [░░░░░░░███]  follow-up, off-season, ads-ready
  ```
- **Plan table:**
  | Phase | Weeks | What we do | What you'll see |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Foundation | 1–2 | Claim Google, shoot real photos, WhatsApp CTA | Hall finally looks active online |
  | Distribution | 3–6 | Weekly reels, review drive, track inquiries | More inquiries, and you know their source |
  | Convert | 7–12 | Follow-up system, off-season activation | More of those inquiries become bookings |
- **Projected trajectory** (if HVT history exists or as a target): a faint line from today's Health Score to the 90-day target band.
- **Takeaway:** *"This is a goal, not a guarantee — but it's the path that consistent work tends to deliver."*

### PAGE 7 — Closing & Next Step
- **Recommendation line** (plain): "What we'd suggest: start with DigiStories (Growth)…" + the chosen plan and the ad-spend disclosure verbatim.
- **Standing disclaimer** (italic): *"These numbers are honest targets based on what consistent work tends to deliver. They are a goal — not a guarantee."*
- **Soft CTA:** "Talk to us on WhatsApp · 91XXXXXXXXXX." No hard sell.

| Chart | Page | Type | Encodes |

| Pillar bars | 2 | horizontal bars, sorted | 5 pillar scores, RAG colour |
| Bullet bars | 3 | bullet chart per metric | You vs area-avg vs leader |
| Competitive dial | 3 | radial dial | Competitive Index + position |
| Revenue waterfall | 4 | waterfall | current → recoverable → potential ₹ |
| Effort–impact 2×2 | 5 | scatter | each quick win (x=effort, y=impact) |
| 90-day timeline | 6 | Gantt bars | 3 phases × weeks |
| Trajectory line | 6 | line | Health Score path to target |

All charts: ivory background, charcoal axes, vermillion for "you/now", gold for "target/leader", muted grey for context. No 3D, no gradients-for-decoration — McKinsey restraint.

## Tables (schemas)
Pillar scorecard · Competitive benchmark · Revenue leakage · Quick wins · 90-day plan (columns defined per page above). All tables: hairline rules only, generous row height, right-aligned numbers, RAG dots not full-cell fills.

## Narrative flow (pyramid principle)
```
Cover → ANSWER (Exec Summary: grade + prize + first move)
      → EVIDENCE (Grade → Benchmark → Weaknesses/Leakage)
      → PLAN (Quick Wins → 90-Day Plan)
      → ASK (Closing recommendation + soft CTA)
```
Situation (you run a strong hall) → Complication (but you're invisible online and leaking ₹X) → Resolution (here's the prioritised 90-day path). Every page ends on a takeaway line so a skimming owner still gets the story.
