The company

Built by someone who
actually ran the banquet.

DigiVenue was started by Rohit Nate — a Maharashtra hospitality operator with over 13 years inside banquet halls, catering and wedding work. Not theory learned from outside. The kind of knowledge that only comes from running the place yourself.

13 years inside the business

Not a startup idea.
Real experience.

Rohit didn't study the wedding business from the outside. He ran banquet halls. He handled bookings, coordinated decorators, sorted out vendor royalty disputes, dealt with GST invoicing, fielded walk-in enquiries — and ran much of it from WhatsApp chats and a paper diary.

He watched good banquets — beautiful interiors, experienced staff, years of goodwill — stay invisible online. Families would search, find a quiet Google page with six-year-old photos, and pick another banquet. The owner never even knew.

SmartOS began as a system he built to run his own banquet. DigiStories began as the visibility work he started doing for banquets that were losing bookings they should have won. The same problems, he realised, were hurting thousands of banquets across India — and nobody had built anything for them.

A banquet can spend crores on its interiors and still lose bookings just because it looks quiet online. Families decide before they call — and most banquets never realise it's happening.
The gap nobody filled

Every platform was built for someone else.

The Indian wedding business has plenty of platforms. None of them were built for the person who runs the banquet.

Listing portals

WedMeGood, Weddingz.in, BookEventz — built for couples, and for commissions. banquets pay to be listed, pay for each enquiry, and have little say in how they're shown. It takes from the banquet more than it gives back.

What owners actually need

A way to track offline leads. A simple system for bookings and payments. Help building online trust over time. And a partner who understands the real chaos of running a banquet — because they've lived it.

The quiet rejection

Families today find banquets through Instagram, Google photos, reviews and reels — before they ever call. Most banquets are strong in person but silent online during exactly this stage. They lose bookings they never even knew they were in the running for.

What DigiVenue does differently

We don't list banquets. We improve how your banquet looks online, build trust over time, and give you tools built around how an Indian banquet hall actually works — from first enquiry to final invoice.
